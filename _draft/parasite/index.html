Parasite by Bong

October 31, 2019 in ML, deep learning, CNN, ECG classfier

밴쿠버에서 살면서 한국 영화를 극장에서 보긴 힘들다. 집에서부터 운전을 한시간은 해야 하고, 자전거로는 두시간 이상이 걸릴 거리에 한인타운 극장이 거의 유일한 곳이다. 기생충은 깐느 영화제에서 큰 상을 받았고 그 덕에 괜찮은 배급사와 북미 계약을 맺었다. 적지 않은 밴쿠버의 극장에서 상영 중이고, 이 영화 취향도 아닐 아내를 졸라 즐겨 찾는 멀티 플렉스 극장을 찾았다.

Kim Ki-taek lives with his wife Chung-sook, son Ki-woo, and daughter Ki-jeong in a shabby semi-basement apartment. The family struggle to survive, working any low-paying gigs available. Ki-woo’s friend Min-hyuk gives them a large rock supposed to bring them wealth before he leaves to study abroad. He suggests Ki-woo pose as a fellow university student to take over his job as an English tutor for the wealthy Park family’s daughter Da-hye. In the lavish Park mansion, Ki-woo learns of Mrs. Park’s struggle to find an art teacher for her younger child Da-song and suggests a “professional” named Jessica, who in reality is Ki-jeong. Once hired, Ki-jeong leaves her underwear in Mr. Park’s car to get his driver fired, then recommends her “uncle’s former driver”: Ki-taek. The Kims next get housekeeper Moon-gwang fired after framing her peach allergy as tuberculosis. Now replaced by Chung-sook, the entire Kim family is employed by the Parks while pretending to be sophisticated skilled workers.

The Parks go on an overnight camping trip, leaving the mansion in Chung-sook’s care. The Kims take the opportunity to have a drunken party there, treating themselves to the home’s luxuries. However, Moon-gwang arrives, claiming that she left something in the basement. The other Kims hide while Chung-sook lets Moon-gwang in. In the basement, the former housekeeper opens a secret bunker, revealing that her husband, Geun-sae, has been hiding for four years to avoid loan sharks.

As Moon-gwang begs Chung-sook to keep the secret, the rest of the Kims, eavesdropping, accidentally stumble into view. Moon-gwang records footage of the new “staff” addressing each other as family, then threatens to expose their scam. Moon-gwang and Geun-sae delight in their blackmail, until a fight erupts and Ki-woo is able to delete the footage.

The Parks suddenly call Chung-sook to announce that the heavy rain has ruined their trip and they will be home soon. The Kims scramble to confine Moon-gwang and Geun-sae, clean up, and hide. Chung-sook pushes Moon-gwang down the stairs, concussing her. When the Parks return, Mrs. Park tells Chung-sook that her son was traumatized years earlier by seeing a “ghost” coming up from the basement: Geun-sae. With the other three Kims hiding nearby, Mr. Park complains to his wife that while Ki-taek is well-behaved, he has a subtly dirty smell – a poor man’s smell. The three Kims leave the mansion undetected, but find their apartment flooded by the storm. They salvage what they can, then sleep in a gym along with other displaced citizens. Meanwhile, Moon-gwang dies from her concussion.

The following day, Mrs. Park throws a birthday party for Da-song and invites the staff and many wealthy friends. During the party, Ki-woo carries the rock his friend gave him down to the bunker, intending to kill Geun-sae. Geun-sae ambushes Ki-woo, cracks his head open with the rock, then crashes the party, where he stabs Ki-jeong in the chest. Geun-sae’s sudden appearance triggers Da-song’s traumatic memory, causing the child to have a seizure. As Ki-taek rushes to stop Ki-jeong’s bleeding, Mr. Park yells at him to drive Da-song to the hospital. Ki-taek throws him the key, which lands under the fighting Chung-sook and Geun-sae, before Chung-sook kills Geun-sae with a meat skewer. As Mr. Park retrieves the key, he recoils from Geun-sae’s smell. Ki-taek, witnessing Mr. Park’s reaction to the smell, which is also his own, snaps, fatally stabs him, then flees.

Ki-jeong and Mr. Park die from their wounds, while Ki-woo requires brain surgery and suffers a weeks-long coma; he and Chung-sook go to trial for fraud and are sentenced to probation. Later, watching the Parks' mansion, Ki-woo notices a light flickering in Morse code – a SKATS message from Ki-taek, who had managed to retreat into the bunker, and has been hiding from and living off of the new owners. Ki-woo promises that one day, he will earn enough money to buy the house and free his father.

That conversation was from total-missing the intention of the director, and that made me so sad again that they could not understand it since they’ve always been enjoying high-class-life. This movie was not at all difficult to understand. Very straightforward description and even important monologue was being repeated several times. Little bit of interest on the unbalance and abusurdities of the capitalism society was all they need to know.

According to film grammar, Mr. Park must have been killed.
